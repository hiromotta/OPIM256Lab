Shown above wearing #17 for the Oklahoma City Thunder is rookie Aleksej Pokuševski. Our club's two most active NBA draft analysts, Hiro Motta & Jay Iraj both favored Poku as a prospect for a wide variety of reasons:

 

  • For his size at 7'0, he displays unicorn-ish fluidity and guard skills including shooting off the dribble, playmaking, and body control with the ball.

  • While he doesn’t have great verticality, his height, instincts, and good hips enable strong rim protection potential and good overall defense.

  • Pokusevski played in the second best league in Greece against weak competition and is extremely skinny, making him a risky bet. However we beleive that NBA teams generally underrate high-ceiling selections in the first round of the draft.

While this is a sample of a more qualitative sense of sports analysis, the Georgetown Sports Analysis Club also engages in quantiative analysis. Using big data arbitrage or alogrithimic analytics, we find mispricings in casual/mainstream sports knowledge.
